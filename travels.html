travels

Traveling across the world has been a significant part of my life. I studied international affairs at Qatar University in Doha, Qatar during the spring semester of 2012. The following summer, I studied Spanish at la Universidad de Salamanca in Salamanca, Spain. While I was overseas, I had the opportunity to travel to the United Arab Emirates, Turkey and Portugal. The following collection of photographs provides a glimpse into some incredibly vibrant and diverse cultures.

Doha, Qatar

Fishing vessels float on the West Bay near downtown Doha.

Doha, Qatar

Designed by I.M. Pei, the Museum of Islamic Art incorporates geometric and floral patterns into the décor.

Doha, Qatar

The sun sets over the West Bay, behind several construction cranes—evidence of the country’s preparation for the 2022 World Cup.

Doha, Qatar

Doha’s ever-expanding skyline includes the striking architecture of the Doha Tower and the Tornado Tower.

Doha, Qatar

Qatar Foundation’s inspirational yellow words can be found all across Doha in English and Arabic.

Cadiz, Spain

Madrid, Spain

Protesters angered by high unemployment march in the streets of Madrid. Shortly after this photo was taken, riot police threw tear gas canisters into the crowd.

Madrid, Spain

Valencia, Spain

The exhibits in the City of Arts and Sciences of Valencia are just as much of an attraction as the stunning and unique architecture.

Istanbul, Turkey

Sultanahmet, the old side of Istanbul, is visible from the Galata Tower, the highest point in the city.

Inside the Ayasofya, or Hagia Sofia, eight giant gold medallions bear the names of Allah, Muhammad, Muhammad’s two grandsons and the first four Caliphs.
